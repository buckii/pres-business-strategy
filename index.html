Business Strategy in a Nutshell

Who do you and don't you serve, in what way, and how?

  • Vision / Mission / Values
  • Value Proposition
  • Positioning

Web Strategy and why you need one

A web strategy translates and extends your offline business/organization strategy to your website, social media presence, email marketing, e-commerce store, and other online communication channels.

An effective web strategy will closely mirror your vision, mission, values, value proposition, competitive advantage, and other strategic imperatives. Before building your website, you should know precisely what purpose it serves as part of your strategy.

5 Steps to Align with Business Strategy

Step 1

Know your Why

Don't skip too quickly to your what and how:
Start with the Why™

  • Vision: A vivid picture of how will our world be better if you are successful
  • Mission: Why and how you will [and won't you] serve your target customers
  • Values: The characteristics and principles that uniquely guide your decisions
  • Positioning: Who you will [and won't] serve relative to competitors

Step 2

Define Profit

Hot Take: The goal of any business is to generate profit.

What is "profit" for your business/organization?

What can a website (or any operational initiative) do to improve profitability for a business?

  • Increase revenue
  • Decrease costs

Step 2

Define Profit

Revenue generated by websites

  • Direct sales (products, services, memberships/subscriptions)
  • Lead generation
  • Fee-based transactions
  • Advertising
  • Affiliate revenue

Step 2

Cost savings through websites

Revenue generated by websites

  • Customer self-service information and tools
  • Error reduction
  • Business process automation
  • Customer intake/onboarding
  • Reduce cost of customer acquisition

Step 3

Know your metrics

Which key performance indicators for your business can you affect with your website?

  • Average Transaction Size
  • Customer Lifetime Value
  • Customer Acquisition Cost
  • Service Delivery Cost

Step 4

Select website goals to drive key metrics

What can a website do to improve profitability for a business?

  • Increase revenue
  • Decrease costs

Step 5

Plan your website around your goals

Select platform, content, functionality, and integrations based on their ability to drive your key metrics.

  • Platform
  • Content
  • Functionality

The most common website goals

E-commerce

sell products or services online

Lead generation

collect information from visitors

Advertising

grow traffic/visitors to view revenue-generating ads

Membership/Community

add active members who will contribute to or use your site's content

Affiliate

advertise and generate sales for other companies' products (earning a % of sales)

Informational/Marketing

inform visitors about your products and services; build credibility; nurture leads

Transactional

conduct business and capture a commission or fee for service

The most common website goals

  • E-commerce - sell products or services online
  • Lead generation - collect information from visitors
  • Advertising - grow traffic/visitors to view revenue-generating ads
  • Membership/Community - add active members who will contribute to or use your site's content
  • Affiliate - advertise and generate sales for other companies' products (earning a % of sales)
